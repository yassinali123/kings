1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites


Copyright is the right of someone who creates an original image. As soon as an image is created it has copyright and the person who created it owns the rights to that image. Only the owner has the right to reproduce the work, display the work in public and copy the work or sell copies of the work to other people. There are trademarks too such as Coke and Pepsi that are protected by law from being used by other people or companies. The only exception to copyright is if you use an image from public domains such as Google Images. It is a difficult area because not everyone has given their permission for their photographs or artwork to be reproduced. Fair Use is when you use an image for educational or news reporting purposes or if you have transformed the image or only used part of it. It is always best to ask the owner of the image if you are not sure for their permission. I think it is better to use your own photographs where possible because then you know they are your own property.
